<Section icon={Shield} title="Digital Defense & Hack Prevention">

As you browse the darknet, rival hackers will target your IP address. When an intrusion is detected, an alarm will sound, and you must immediately switch to your defense software. Failing these mini-games results in a loss of DOS Coins, system exposure, or an instant death. There are two primary hacking threats you must defend against: the Stack Overflow attack and the Memory D-Allocator challenge.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Detect the Intrusion">
    Listen for the high-pitched alarm or watch for screen distortion, indicating a hacker is actively breaching your system.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Initialize Defense Tools">
    Open the corresponding defensive application from your desktop immediately. Do not hesitate, as you only have a few seconds to react.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute Defensive Inputs">
    For the Memory D-Allocator, look at the bottom line of numbers. If the left section turns green, press the Left arrow. If the right turns green, press Right. If both are green, press Space.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Secure the System">
    For the Stack Pusher, use the pusher node (diamond) to push the active stack nodes (squares) into the center three-pronged node. Completing the defense steals DOS Coins from the hacker.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Defending your system is not just about survival; it is your primary source of income. Successfully blocking a hack awards you with the hacker's own DOS Coins, which you can use to purchase utility items from the Dark Drop storefront. These items include the Backdoor Hack auto-defense tool and the Shadow Fetch downloader, both of which are essential for surviving the later hours of the night.

| Program Name | Primary Threat | Key Input Mechanics |
|---|---|---|
| Stack Pusher | Stack Overflow Attack | Move diamond to push squares into the center |
| Memory D-Allocator | Memory Deallocation | Match directional keys with the green bar |
| Backdoor Hack | Direct System Breach | Consumable item that automatically blocks a hack |

<Callout type="warning" title="Anti-Panic Protocol">

<Section icon={Swords} title="Physical Threat Mitigation: Patrick and the Kidnapper">

Surviving the digital landscape is only half the battle. You must also defend your physical space from two deadly intruders: Patrick and the Kidnapper. Patrick is a silent stalker who watches you from inside your room. He does not make noise, but his presence is indicated by a shadowy human silhouette cast on the wall or corkboard directly above your computer monitor.

To detect Patrick, you must regularly right-click to look around your room. If you spot his shadow, you must immediately stop moving your mouse. Remaining completely still simulates playing dead, causing Patrick to eventually retreat. If you move your camera even slightly while he is watching, he will instantly attack and end your run.
